Potential Energy at Secondary Level

Potential energy is a key concept in physics that deals with stored energy. At the secondary level, we explore this energy form, which is often associated with the position of an object in a force field.

The formula for gravitational potential energy (\(PE_{\text{gravity}}\)) near the Earth's surface is given by:

\[ PE_{\text{gravity}} = m \cdot g \cdot h \]

Where: \( m \) is the mass of the object, \( g \) is the acceleration due to gravity (approximately 9.8 m/sÂ²), and \( h \) is the height above a reference point.
